Discover the unexplored gems of Delhi NCR and make your journey unforgettable!

Mangar Bani

Mangar Bani is a beautiful and ecologically important forest located near the village of Mangar, in the Aravalli hills, between Gurgaon and Faridabad, Haryana. Just about 30–40 km from Delhi, this ancient grove is known for its dense greenery, rare flora and fauna, and sacred spiritual significance.

Bhardwaj Lake

Bhardwaj Lake, nestled within the Asola Bhatti Wildlife Sanctuary near Tughlakabad, is a stunning hidden spot in Delhi. Formed from an abandoned mining quarry, the lake is surrounded by rocky cliffs and greenery. It’s a peaceful place for trekking, birdwatching, and perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Agrasen Ki Baoli

Agrasen ki Baoli is a centuries-old stepwell quietly nestled near Connaught Place. With deep stone steps and hauntingly beautiful architecture, it offers a unique blend of history and mystery. Though surrounded by modern buildings, it remains a peaceful, photogenic spot, perfect for history.

Tughlaqabad Fort

Tughlaqabad Fort, located on the outskirts of Delhi, is a massive yet lesser-visited ruined fort from the 14th century. Built by Ghiyas-ud-din Tughlaq, it offers stunning views, mysterious pathways. it’s perfect for explorers, history lovers, and those craving offbeat adventures.

Sanjay Van

Sanjay Van is a vast, forested area near Vasant Kunj and Mehrauli, offering a peaceful escape within Delhi. Spread over 780 acres, it’s rich in greenery, wildlife, and old ruins. Ideal for morning walks, birdwatching, and quiet reflection, this hidden forest is a secret haven for nature lovers.

Surajkund

Surajkund, located near Faridabad, is an ancient sun-shaped reservoir built in the 10th century. Surrounded by arid hills and natural beauty, it's a peaceful spot rarely crowded except during the Surajkund Mela. It’s perfect for a quiet picnic, photography, or exploring heritage away from city buzz.
